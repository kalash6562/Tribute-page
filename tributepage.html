Time line of Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel

1875

Sardar Patel was born in October,31st of 1875 in Nadiad (Currently in Gujarat State). His native village was Karamsad. He was born to a land owning farmer family.

1893

He was married to Jhaverba of Gana village at the age 18.

1897

Sardar Patel passed the Matriculation Examns at the age of 22.

1901

He became a district pleader and started practising law at Ghodhra court

1903

Birth of Maniben, who was the daughter of Sardar patel and Jhaverba in 1903.

1905

Birth of son Dahyabhai to Jhaverben and Sardar patel.

1909

Jhaverba died in a hospital in Bombay. He got the message through telegram, still he completed the case proceedings in interest of person being defended.

1910

He sent Maniben and Dahyabhai to St. Mary school of Bombay. He then enrolled in middle temple inn in London for Barristership. He finished the 36 months course in 30 months and had top in his class with a price received 50 pounds.

1913

Returning to India from England he setteled in Ahmedabad and became one of the most successful barristers. He became Criminal lawyer whic made him the one of the wealthiest Barristers in Ahmedabad.

1915

He became member of Gujarat Sabha which later called Gujarat Prantic Samiti. Gujarat Sabha nominated him as delagate to attend Indian National Congress meeting in Mumbai.(December 7, 1915).

1917

He was elected as member of Ahmedabad Municipality on January 5, 1917 from Driyapur ward by one vote. This was his first foray in an election of a political insitution. This election was challenged and get cancelled. He was elected again as an uncontested member on May 14, 1917 and became chairman of Sanitation Committee.

The first conference was held in Godhra under the chairmanship of Mahatma Dandhi and Vallabhbhai Patel was appointed as its Secretary. They fought against the custom of forced labour.

1918 - Kheda Satyagraha

The Kheda Satyagraha was organised to support the peasants of Kheda district who were unable to pay the high taxes leveid by the british due to crop failure and a plaugue epidemic. Sardar Patel and a group Mahatma Gandhi's supporters organised a major tax revolt, and all the different ethnic and caste communities of Kheda rallied around it. The peasants of Kheda signed the petition calling for the tax for that year to be scrapped, in the wake of a famine. The Government first retaliated by rejecting the charter and confiscating properties, but finally relented for an honourable agreement for both the parties. The tax for the year and the next would be suspended and the increase in interest rate reduced also the consficated properties were returned.

The victory was celebrated in Nadiad.

1920

Congress won most of the municipal seats in Ahmedabad due to Vallabhbhai's efforts.

Vallabhbhai moved the resolution in Ahmedabad Municipality to place a statue of Bal Gangadhar tilak Maharaj in Victoria Garden on Jan 10, 1921. It was installed in 1929 after Sardar Patel left the Ahmedabad Municipality.

1923-Flag Satyagraha

He led the Nagpur Flag Satyagraha to uphold the right to honour national flag when Mahatma Gandhi was in jail.

1924-Elected as President and Borsad Satyagraha

Sardar Vallabhabhai Patel won majority for Congress in the Ahmedabad Municipal body and was lected its president. He undertook several development projects within the walled city of Ahmedabad and also encouraged the extension beyond its existing limits.

He also led the Borsad Satyagraha and won during this period.

1927-Gujarat faces unprecedented floods

When Gujarat faced unprecedented flooods, Sardar worked tirelessly and organised extensive relief works and visited various sites. He did not go home six days and stayed in the office. He also extended support to District Collector of Kheda for relief operations.

1928-Resigned from Presidentship and Bardoli Satyagraha

On April 14, 1928, Sardar resigned from Presidentship of Ahmedabad Municipality following differences over appointment of Chief Officer, I.R. Bhagat who faced allegations of corruption. Sardar Patel never returned to Ahmedabad Municipality in spite of several requests.

The Bardoli Satyagraha of 1928 was led by Vallabhbhai Patel and was a aclassic example of Civil Disobedience Satyagraha and revolt in the Indian Independece Movement. It established Sardar Ptel as one of the main leaders of the Independece Movement. It galvanised peasants of bardoli to protest against arbitrary increase in land revenue and launch a satyagraha. Peasants did not yield to coercion even though Government consfiscated their land, property, crops and cattle. Government has to yeild to the demand of appointment of Inquiry Committee to review increased land revenue and the protest was thus successful. Vallabhbahi Patel was named as Sardar, the leader, in the hearts of people.

1939-Dandi March

Mahatma Gandhi historic Salt March to Dandi is one of the landmark of Indian Independence Movement. Sardar Patel was arrested on March 1, 1930 at Ras village near Anand where of he was deliver a lecture to rouse the conscience of people in favour of the salt satyagraha.

July, 31- Sardar Patel was arrested and sentenced to three months' imprisonment, when he joined a procession in Bombay on Tilak anniversary and addressed a vast public gathering at Azad Maidan, Bombay. He was again arrested and sentenced to nine months' imprisonment while delivering a alecture opposing Government plicy. On Novenmber 1, he wa released from jail. Sardar Patel was jailed 6 times in his entire lifetime.

1931-President, Indian National Congress

He became the president of Indian National Congress in its Karachi session. He praised the martyrs of the indian freedom struggle-Bhagat Singh, Sukhdev and Rajguru. He remained at the forefront of the party and had a decisive control till independence.

1932-Nasik Prison

Sardar Patel eas shifted from Yervada to Nashik prison. His mother Laadba died while he was in jail.

His elder brother Vithalbhai died in 1933 and yet Sardar did not submit to conditions of British authorities for a parole to attend the funeral.

1938-Session of Congress under the guidance of Sardar Patel

Feb 19 to 21, the session of Congress was held at Haripura in Surat District under the guidance of Sardar. Subhash Chandra Bose was elected as the President of the Congress.

1939-Prajamandal conference

In May while attending Prajamandal Conference at Bhavnagar, Sardar was attacked by a minority group near Nagina Masjid, he was rescued but two of his associates were killed

1940-Sardar's Arrest

Arrested in Ahmedabad during the individual Satyagraha launced to protest for upholding the freedom of speech.

1941-Released from prison

He was released from prison due to ailment of intestines.

1942

'Quit India' Resolution was passed by Bombay session of All India Congress Committee(AICC). On the morning of August9, 1942 Sardar Patel was detained and sent to Ahmednagar jail and was later released. All India Congress leaders were released for the pre-Independece negotiations after almost three years.

1947

July 05, -A ministry for negotiationg with princely states was created and sardar was sworn in as Minister. Sardar persuaded princely states to sign Standstill Agreement and Instrument of Accession within a short period. Thus, he succeeded in bringing India altogether as One Nation under one system of governance for the first time in her history.

August 15-India achieved independence and Sardar was sworn in as Home Minister and Deputy Prime Minister.

October 26,Maharaj Harisingh accepted Kashmir into the Indian Union and Sardar Patel sent the Indian Army to Kashmir.

Novenmber 9-Junagadh Dispute:

Ignored Lord Mountbatten's advice to refer Junagadh issue to UNO and persuaded the Government of India to accept the accession of Junagadh State.

Novenmber 13-Visisted Somnath Temple

Sardar-Vallabhbhai-Patel visited somnath temple in Saurastra.
Resolved to rebuild it with public funds.

1948

September 24 Operation Polo(Police action) was completed in Hydrabad and Nizam acceded Hydrabad into the Indian Union.

1950

January 26-India became a Republic with Dr.Rajendra Prasad, as its first president and Pandit Jawarharlal Nehru as its first Prime Minister
December 15-Breathed his last in Mumbai.
Sardar Patel took his last breath at 9:37 AM, in Mumbai. Cremated in Sonapur (Marine Lines, Mumbai).
December 28- His ashes were immersed in holy confluence at Prayag.
